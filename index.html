  <div class="sec-label">From Potential to Discipline</div>

  <h2 class="sec-title">Identity, Brand &amp; Reputation</h2>

  <p class="persona-intro">Throughout BCOM 314, I collected structured data on how I perceive myself versus how others perceive me — using a Self-Perception Inventory (SPI), a Professional Reputation Survey (3 respondents), and team 360° evaluations. The SPI is a scored self-assessment measuring attitudinal communication style and work environment preferences. The Professional Reputation Survey asks people in your network to rate your behavioral communication tendencies and select adjectives that describe you. Together, these tools answer one key question: to what extent does my self-perception align with how others perceive me?</p>


  <!-- 1. CORE VALUES -->

  <div class="persona-block">

    <h2 class="sub-h">Core Values: The Foundation of My Brand</h2>

    <p class="sub-p">My three core values — Discipline, Accountability, and Growth — appear consistently across both my self-perception and the adjectives others selected to describe me. Respondents who chose "Hard-Working," "Driven," and "Perfectionistic" are confirming externally what I hold as my internal identity. This alignment is the foundation of a credible, authentic professional brand.</p>

    <div class="cv-grid">

      <div class="cv-card"><div class="cv-icon">⚡</div><h4>Discipline</h4><p>Consistent effort and daily habits separate average from exceptional. This value shows up in my 3.63 GPA, my Dean's List record, and how I approach every internship and leadership role.</p></div>

      <div class="cv-card"><div class="cv-icon">🎯</div><h4>Accountability</h4><p>I take ownership of my results. My future is a direct reflection of my actions — a belief that drives my academic performance, career decisions, and personal development.</p></div>

      <div class="cv-card"><div class="cv-icon">📈</div><h4>Growth</h4><p>I actively seek challenges that force me to evolve. Earlier in college I was comfortable being average — that changed. I now embrace difficulty as the fastest path to becoming better.</p></div>

    </div>

  <div class="persona-block">

    <p class="sub-p">The SPI classifies communication style along two behavioral axes: Composite Proactivity/Assertiveness (x-axis) and Composite Emotiveness/Reactivity (y-axis). Scores to the right of center indicate higher proactivity; scores above center indicate higher expressiveness. My results place me at the Driver/Expressive border — confirmed by how others rated me.</p>

    <div class="comm-grid">

      <div class="comm-card"><div class="comm-lbl">Professional Reputation Survey (3 respondents)</div><h4>Driver</h4><p>Proactivity: 5.0 &nbsp;·&nbsp; Reactivity: −0.3. Others perceive me as significantly more proactive than I rate myself — and even more emotionally steady. My outward presence reads louder than I feel internally.</p></div>

    </div>

    <p class="sub-p" style="margin-bottom:0;"><strong style="color:var(--navy);">Key insight:</strong> The gap between my self-rated proactivity (2.0) and others' perception (5.0) is the most striking finding in my data. I experience myself as moderately assertive, but colleagues observe a highly decisive, take-charge communicator. This is a strength in financial services — but I must balance it by creating deliberate space for others to contribute.</p>

    <div class="gm-box"><h4>Where I Started</h4><p>In my first year, I prioritized comfort over challenge. I completed assignments without pushing myself to deeply understand the material or exceed expectations. My mindset was task-oriented rather than growth-oriented.</p></div>

    <div class="gm-box"><h4>The Shift</h4><p>Feedback from my instructor and teammates pushed me to be more intentional — to clearly explain my thinking, avoid surface-level analysis, and fully understand concepts rather than just completing tasks. That feedback was uncomfortable at first, but it catalyzed a real change in how I approach academic and professional work.</p></div>

    <div class="gm-box"><h4>Where I Am Now &amp; What I'm Actively Improving</h4><p>I now embrace challenges as the fastest path to growth. The specific skill I am most intentionally developing is the ability to communicate complex ideas clearly and professionally in high-stakes settings — whether presenting to a client, pitching to an employer, or leading a team through ambiguity.</p></div>

  <div class="reflection">

    <h3>Moving Forward: Closing the Gap</h3>

    <p>The most important finding from this semester's data is not where my identity and reputation differ — it is how closely they already align. My core values of Discipline, Accountability, and Growth appear consistently across both my self-perception and what others observe in me. That alignment is the foundation of a credible professional brand.</p>

    <p>The gap I plan to close is on the listening and curiosity dimension. Feedback consistently points to a tendency to lead rather than inquire — a strength in high-stakes environments, but one that limits depth in collaborative and client-facing settings. In wealth management, where understanding a client's complete financial picture requires patient, empathetic inquiry, this is a skill I will actively develop.</p>

    <p><strong style="color:var(--gold-light);">My professional development goals for the next 1–2 years:</strong> Gain hands-on experience in wealth management or financial advising. Build technical expertise in investment analysis and portfolio strategy. And develop into a more structured, confident communicator — one who can translate complex financial information into clear, compelling narratives for any audience.</p>
